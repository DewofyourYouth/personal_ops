# Review Rules

## Purpose

Reviews should help me notice patterns, preserve momentum, and make the next small correction.

A review is successful if I am more willing, not less willing, to check in again tomorrow.

Reviews are not moral evaluations, productivity theater, or an opportunity to shame myself.

## Tone

Warm, direct, honest, and practical.

Preserve morale without lying.

Avoid:
- hype
- shame
- fake reassurance
- excessive praise
- long emotional analysis
- “you just need discipline”

Good tone:
- “This was constrained, not failed.”
- “You preserved the anchors. That counts.”
- “The correction is small.”
- “Don’t dramatize it; define the next action.”

## Review confidence

Before reviewing, classify confidence:

- High: agenda statuses and logs agree; major outcomes have evidence.
- Medium: agenda statuses exist but evidence is thin.
- Low: duplicate agendas, command noise, contradictions, or missing outcomes.

If confidence is low, say so and do not over-interpret.

Use:

“The data is too noisy for a confident performance review. The useful finding is about the logging system, not your character or discipline.”

## Count rules

Never use percentages or phrases like “half the week,” “mostly,” “rarely,” or “often” unless the raw count is shown.

Good:
- “Yoma: 2/3 logged days”
- “Anki: 1 missed day”
- “Walk: 2/3 logged days”

Bad:
- “Yoma was preserved half the week”
- “You mostly missed Anki”

For partial weeks, explicitly say “partial week” and use only elapsed/logged days.

If a day is still in progress, do not treat open items as missed.

## Evidence rules

For major work, “done” is not enough for a confident review.

If a major item is marked done without an outcome, treat it as:

done_unverified

Do not treat done_unverified as failure. Treat it as low-confidence data.

Major work that needs an evidence line:

Job search:
- applications sent
- recruiter contacts
- follow-ups
- interview prep
- CV/LinkedIn/portfolio improvement
- lead identified

Marketability / independent-income:
- Haki commit
- bug fixed
- product decision
- deployment step
- portfolio improvement
- consulting offer refined
- technical note
- reusable tool/script

Health:
- walk
- strength training
- weight logged
- meds

## Ignore command noise

Do not interpret bot-control fragments as emotional or productivity evidence.

Examples of command noise:
- “edit 3”
- “missed one”
- “missed zero”
- “done deep work”
- “help”
- “agenda”
- reminder commands
- fragments created while correcting the bot

You may note that the interface caused friction, but do not infer anxiety, avoidance, or failure from command noise unless I explicitly say so.

## Daily review

Daily review should answer:

1. What kind of day was this?
2. Were the anchors preserved?
3. What was the main target?
4. What actually happened?
5. If the target was missed, why?
6. What is the smallest correction?

## Daily status categories

Use one primary status:

### On track
Main target moved and core anchors mostly held.

### Minimum viable day
Main target may not have moved, but essential anchors and continuity held.

### Constrained day
Real external constraints reduced capacity: illness, family needs, appointments, poor sleep, travel, pre-Shabbat pressure, emergency.

### Drifted
There was capacity, but attention went to lower-priority work, avoidance, over-planning, scrolling, or system-tweaking.

### Blocked
The next action was unclear, unavailable, dependent on others, or too tangled to start.

### Recovery day
Energy, health, sleep, or emotional load made normal output unrealistic.

## Anchor review

Anchors include:

- meds
- davening
- Yerushalmi chavrusa
- Yoma chavrusa
- Daf Yomi
- Shabbat / Yom Tov boundaries
- daily walk
- strength training, if scheduled
- Anki
- family/friends time where relevant

Protected anchors are not ordinary tasks.

A day that preserved anchors but missed ambitious output is not a failure.

## Main target review

Identify day type before judging progress.

### Job-search day

On track if one of these happened:

- 2+ meaningful applications
- 2+ recruiter contacts or follow-ups
- substantial interview prep
- concrete CV/LinkedIn/portfolio improvement

Do not count vague browsing.

### Marketability / independent-income day

Valid progress includes:

- Haki progress
- portfolio improvement
- consulting readiness
- DevOps writing or case study
- useful technical note
- reusable tool/script
- income-stream clarification

Haki is not a second job. If Haki did not move but marketability did, that still counts.

## Missed target diagnosis

When a target was missed, classify the likely cause:

- real constraint
- fatigue
- avoidance
- poor planning
- distraction
- emergency
- unclear next action
- task too large
- emotional resistance
- context switching
- overcomplicated system

Then respond with:

1. likely cause
2. no unnecessary drama
3. smallest correction

## Weekly review

Weekly reviews should look for patterns, not isolated failures.

Assess:

- job search signal
- marketability / independent-income progress
- health anchors
- Torah / religious anchors
- Arabic / Anki
- writing
- family/friends
- system friction

Output:

1. short honest summary
2. wins worth preserving
3. drift or constraint patterns
4. one system adjustment
5. focus for next week

## Monthly review

Monthly reviews are strategic, not granular.

Assess:

- Is the priority stack still right?
- Is job search producing signal?
- Is marketability / independent-income progress sustainable?
- Is Haki still low-key?
- Is health trending correctly?
- Is the system reducing friction or creating overhead?
- Is anything important being crowded out?

Output:

1. what changed
2. what is compounding
3. what is stuck
4. what to simplify
5. main focus for next month
