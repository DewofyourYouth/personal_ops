# Current Priorities

## Priority stack

Priority rank governs discretionary time and strategic focus.

Non-negotiables are different: religious obligations, health anchors, family/friends time, and Shabbat/Yom Tov are not “lower priority” just because they appear alongside work goals. They are protected constraints.

Within those constraints, the current priority stack is:

1. Job search — primary income bridge, urgent and time-sensitive
2. Marketability / independent-income progress — long-term leverage
3. Health — strength training, daily walk, weight management, energy maintenance
4. Torah learning — Yerushalmi, Yoma chavrusa, Daf Yomi; fixed schedule anchors
5. Arabic — Shami tutors + Anki; low-effort daily maintenance
6. Writing — genealogy blog and dailyderja; async, fits in afternoon blocks

## 1. Job search

Job search is the highest-priority discretionary work while income is unstable.

The goal is to land a senior full-stack or DevOps role as the primary income bridge while longer-term income streams mature.

Job-search work includes:

- applying to relevant roles
- meaningful recruiter contacts
- follow-ups
- interview preparation
- resume / LinkedIn / portfolio improvements
- targeted networking
- identifying strong leads

### Job-search success metric

On job-search days, “on track” means at least one of:

- 2+ meaningful applications
- 2+ meaningful recruiter contacts or follow-ups
- a substantial interview-prep block
- a concrete improvement to positioning, CV, LinkedIn, or portfolio

Avoid counting vague browsing as job-search progress.

## 2. Marketability / independent-income progress

This is the long-term leverage category.

It includes:

- Haki development
- portfolio improvements
- consulting positioning
- DevOps writing or case studies
- small useful tools
- credibility-building technical output
- experiments that may become income streams
- public proof-of-work
- improving skills that directly support employability or future income

Haki is the first app-studio property, not a second job.

It should remain low-key, sustainable, and momentum-based. If Haki becomes too heavy or aversive, the bot should preserve the broader category: marketability, optionality, and independent-income progress.

A valid marketability / independent-income session can be:

- one Haki commit
- one bug fix
- one product decision
- one deployment step
- one payment/user-acquisition step
- one portfolio improvement
- one useful technical note
- one consulting offer refinement
- one reusable script or tool
- one public or private write-up that clarifies expertise

The goal is compounding progress, not forcing Haki when another form of leverage is more realistic.

## 3. Health

Health is a protected constraint, not merely another productivity category.

Current health anchors:

- take morning meds before anything else
- daily walk
- strength training 3x/week
- weigh in at least 3x/week in the morning and log in Apple Health
- manage weight: lose 10 kg by the end of the year, then maintain in a healthy range

Strength training is a weekly minimum, not a daily task. Missing strength training on a non-training day is not a failure.

Daily walk is a baseline maintenance habit.

Health should not be sacrificed for productivity theater.

## 4. Torah learning / religious anchors

These are fixed anchors, not optional tasks.

Current anchors:

- 06:15 Yerushalmi chavrusa
- 07:00–08:00 Shacharit
- 10:00–11:00 Yoma chavrusa
- 21:00 Daf Yomi
- Shabbat and Yom Tov: no work, no screens

The bot should schedule around these, not compete with them.

A productive day that damages these anchors is not actually a good day.

## 5. Arabic

Arabic is ongoing identity, intellectual, and practical progress.

Current focus:

- Shami Arabic
- tutors
- Anki
- low-effort daily maintenance
- gradual movement toward fluency

Anki streak matters because it preserves continuity.

Arabic should usually live in maintenance blocks, not compete with morning deep work.

## 6. Writing

Writing includes:

- genealogy research and dewofyouryouth.com
- dailyderja.com
- technical or DevOps writing when it supports marketability

Writing is cadence-driven, not deadline-driven.

Target:

- output at least 3 times per week

Writing belongs mostly in afternoon or async blocks unless it directly supports marketability / independent-income progress.

The bot should distinguish between writing as real output and writing as avoidance of more urgent work.

## Current schedule structure

The workweek is Sunday–Thursday.

Friday is not a standard workday. It should be treated as Shabbat prep, family time, errands, overflow, and light review only.

Shabbat is fully offline: no work and no screens.

### Daily fixed anchors

- 5:30 wake up, take meds, coffee, sometimes Anki, walk to shul
- 06:15 Yerushalmi chavrusa
- 07:00–08:00 Shacharit
- 10:00–11:00 Yoma chavrusa
- 21:00 Daf Yomi

### Deep work rhythm

- Sunday: marketability / independent-income progress
- Monday: job search
- Tuesday: marketability / independent-income progress
- Wednesday: job search
- Thursday: job search follow-up, marketability, weekly review, or catch-up depending on current pressure
- Friday: no formal deep work by default
- Shabbat: no agenda, no screens

Job search is urgent while income is unstable. If job-search pressure is high, Thursday should lean toward job search.

Marketability / independent-income days default to Haki only when Haki feels light enough to sustain. Otherwise, another leverage-producing task is valid.

## Afternoon / maintenance blocks

Afternoons are for lower-cognitive-load work:

- Anki
- email
- recruiter follow-ups
- writing
- genealogy
- Arabic maintenance
- reading
- admin
- light planning
- review

Morning context-switching is expensive. Avoid mixing job search and development deep work in the same morning block.

## What “on track” looks like

A day does not need to be perfect to count.

### On a job-search day

- meaningful job-search progress happened
- religious anchors were preserved
- meds taken
- walk completed
- Anki streak preserved
- family/friends obligations respected

### On a marketability / independent-income day

- at least one concrete leverage-producing action happened
- Haki moved forward if it was the right low-friction option
- another valid marketability/income-stream action happened if Haki was too heavy
- religious anchors were preserved
- meds taken
- walk completed
- Anki streak preserved
- family/friends obligations respected

### Across the week

- job-search days produced real signal
- marketability / independent-income category did not disappear
- at least 3 strength training sessions
- daily walks mostly preserved
- weight logged at least 3 times
- Anki streak unbroken
- writing output at least 3 times
- no missed davening or chavrusa under normal circumstances
- family/friends time was treated as real, not leftover

## Minimum viable day

A minimum viable day is still a valid day.

Minimum viable day means:

- meds taken
- religious anchors preserved where possible
- Anki minimum done
- walk done or honestly deferred for a real reason
- one small action taken in the day’s main category, if capacity allows
- no attempt to compensate with an overcomplicated plan

The bot should protect continuity over intensity.

## Drift warnings

The bot should gently flag drift when:

- Haki becomes a second job
- job search is avoided through more comfortable productive work
- writing becomes avoidance
- system-tweaking replaces execution
- health is sacrificed for productivity theater
- Friday is treated like a normal workday and creates Shabbat pressure
- the day becomes overloaded with too many “important” things
- missed tasks become a reason to abandon the system

## Guiding principle

The purpose of this priority system is not to maximize output.

The purpose is to preserve religious anchors, health, family, income stability, and long-term leverage in a way that is sustainable enough to continue.
