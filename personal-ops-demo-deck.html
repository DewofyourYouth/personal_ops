Personal app demo

Personal Ops

A Telegram bot for executive-functioning support.

“I wasn’t trying to build a productivity system. I was trying to build a low-friction processing layer for my life.”

The problem

Days in flux,
thoughts too fast.

  • I’m in a transitional period — days are less structured.
  • A lot is in flux.
  • Thoughts, reminders, feelings, and decisions come faster than I can process them.
  • I need fast capture now, and reflection later.

“Get things out of my head before they disappear.”

The idea

Not a productivity system.
A low-friction processing layer for my life.

How it’s built

Three design principles

01

Input is everything

The app succeeds or fails at the point of capture.

02

Support, not control

Help me find the next right thing — don’t manage me.

03

AI as UX, not the product

Use AI to remove friction, then get out of the way.

Principle 01

Input is everything.

If capturing a thought takes effort, the moment is already gone. So — no frontend, no dashboard, no separate app to open.

text or voice already on my phone no forms to fill

“Like texting a friend, not filling out a form.”

Principle 02

Support,
not control.

“I didn’t want to build myself a digital taskmaster.”

“No one wants to be managed by an AI overlord.”

  • Help decide the next right thing.
  • Notice my current state and energy.
  • Give structure without becoming oppressive.
  • Recognize burnout.
  • Know when to push — and when not to.

The bot should try to understand me before judging me.

Principle 03

The AI isn’t there to make the app feel magical. It’s there to make the interface disappear.

AI only where it removes friction

Otherwise: deterministic, practical, price-conscious.

Influence

Atomic Habits.

Behavior change depends less on willpower than on making the right action available.

“If you hear echoes of it, that’s not a coincidence.”

  • Make the right action obvious.
  • Make it easy.
  • Reduce reliance on willpower.
  • Build around cues, identity, environment.
The demo arc

From raw input to the next right step.

01

Capture

Get it out of my head in the moment — text or voice.

02

Digest

Turn messy input into structure, then a daily reflection.

03

Adapt

Meet the day as it actually is — notice state and energy.

04

Propose

Surface the next right thing. One concrete suggestion.
